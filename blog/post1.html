fasTBSEQ: Long vs Short Reads for TB Surveillance

June 2026

One of the projects I am currently involved in is fasTBSEQ, an initiative focused on advancing genomic surveillance of Mycobacterium tuberculosis in Catalonia. This work builds on the ongoing TBSEQCAT programme, which works closely with public health teams across the region.

TBSEQCAT has already demonstrated the impact of genomic data in understanding transmission dynamics. For example, recent work identified outbreak clusters in school settings (Prieto-García et al., 2026), alongside several ongoing investigations.

The fasTBSEQ project, funded by the La Marató de TV3 Respiratory Disease call, aims to expand sequencing capacity and improve analysis pipelines for tuberculosis surveillance.

A key focus is comparing short-read and long-read sequencing technologies. Long-read approaches, particularly nanopore sequencing, offer improved resolution of complex genomic regions and more complete genome assemblies (Schiffer et al., 2025).

By integrating these approaches into a real public health framework, fasTBSEQ aims to improve outbreak detection, transmission tracking, and decision-making.
