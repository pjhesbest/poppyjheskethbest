Intro

I am a bioinformatician and microbial ecologist with a PhD in Marine Biology (Microbiology) from the University of Plymouth. My research focuses on understanding complex biological systems through high-throughput sequencing, metagenomics, and evolutionary analysis.

I have worked internationally across the UK, USA, and Spain, studying diverse systems including deep-sea sponges, Antarctic invertebrates, coral reef microbiomes, honey bee viruses, and human pathogens. Currently, I am a Bioinformatics Investigator at IGTP (Barcelona), where I work on pathogen genomic surveillance, with a focus on Mycobacterium tuberculosis and real-time epidemiology.

My interests lie at the intersection of microbial ecology, viral evolution, and genomic epidemiology. Through my research, I aim to integrate bioinformatics with experimental approaches to better understand host–microbe interactions and apply these insights to both environmental and public health challenges. You can explore some of my work here.

Work

IGTP – Institut Germans Trias i Pujol, Spain

Postdoctoral Research Associate / Bioinformatician (2024 – Present)
Bioinformatician in the Pathogen Diagnostics and Genomic Epidemiology (DxEpiPath) group, developing and maintaining pipelines for genomic surveillance of SARS‑CoV‑2, Mycobacterium tuberculosis, HIV, and Hepatitis C. My work focuses on advancing bioinformatic methods for pathogen genomics, alongside manuscript and grant writing.

University of Minnesota, USA

Postdoctoral Research Associate (2023 – 2024)
Investigated the genomics of honey bee RNA viruses (BARDS-funded project). Developed automated Oxford Nanopore pipelines and conducted large-scale genomic surveys and recombination analyses of bee-associated viruses.

University of Miami, USA

Postdoctoral Research Associate (2022 – 2023)
Studied viral ecology in anoxic lake systems and horizontal gene transfer across bacteria, plasmids, and bacteriophages using Hi-C and metagenomics. Contributed to grant proposals, publications, and mentored PhD students.

British Antarctic Survey, UK

Postdoctoral Research Assistant (2021 – 2022)
Conducted metagenomic sequencing of Antarctic invertebrates (NERC-funded project), benchmarking Oxford Nanopore technologies and exploring microbial consortia for novel secondary metabolites with biotechnological potential.
